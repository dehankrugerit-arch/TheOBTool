Observation Checklist for Rigid Body Dump Truck Operation

1. Observation Checklist

Specific Outcome 1: Demonstrate Knowledge of the Functions of a Rigid Body Dump Truck

Assessment Criteria 1.1: The main function of the Rigid Body Dump Truck is explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Explain primary purpose: To haul and dump bulk materials such as overburden or ore safely on mining or construction sites, utilizing the rigid chassis for stability on haul roads.
  2. Identify key features: Tipping body (e.g., 50-ton capacity), exhaust brake for controlled descent; ensure hoist cylinder is secured to prevent unintended dumps.
  3. Describe limits: Max payload (e.g., 40 tons), ground clearance (0.8m); reference charts to avoid overload tipping on grades up to 10%.
  4. Integrate safety: Functions comply with site protocols to prevent spills during tipping or collisions in convoy operations.
  5. Visual demo: Point to cab controls and body to illustrate load cycle without engine start, emphasizing tailgate latch verification.

Assessment Criteria 1.2: Rigid Body Dump Truck operation procedures are listed, and the main functions of all major components are explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. List procedures: Pre-trip inspection, loading alignment, haul route adherence, safe dumping, post-trip log; sequence to ensure no overload or route deviations.
  2. Explain engine: High-torque diesel (e.g., 500hp) for hill climbs; check oil (SAE 15W-40) and air filter to prevent power loss.
  3. Explain fuel system: Dual tanks (1000L total); inspect for leaks and sediment to maintain fuel flow during long hauls.
  4. Explain hydraulic/hoist system: Tips body (300 bar pressure); monitor fluid (ISO VG 46) and rams for safe, controlled dumps.
  5. Explain electrical system: Dash indicators, retarder activation; test battery (24V) and lights for night operations.
  6. Explain braking/retarder: Secondary exhaust brake for descents; calibrate service brakes for 5% grade stops without fade.
  7. Explain cooling system: Large radiator for engine/transmission; flush weekly to avoid overheating in hot climates.

Assessment Criteria 1.3: Maximum operating capacities of the Rigid Body Dump Truck are described in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Locate capacity plate: On door frame, specifying GVW (e.g., 90 tons) to guide loading scales accurately.
  2. Describe body capacity: Heaped 45 m³ for ore; derate 10% for sticky materials to prevent carryback.
  3. Explain load charts: Torque limits at 8% grade; maintain 80% payload for safety margin.
  4. On-site verify: Weigh load, adjust for tire pressure (6 bar) to ensure even distribution.
  5. Transport specs: Axle loads compliant with road regs; lower body for low-bed trailer moves.

Specific Outcome 2: Start and Shut Down Rigid Body Dump Truck

Assessment Criteria 2.1: Pre-operational checks are carried out according to the daily inspection checklist.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Don PPE: Hard hat, hi-vis, steel boots, gloves; check for tears to protect from dust/debris during walks.
  2. Walk-around: Inspect tires (tread min 20mm), body hinges for cracks, lights/mirrors; scan underbody for leaks.
  3. Engine compartment: Oil/fuel/coolant levels; clean intake, check fan belts for tension.
  4. Cab: Seatbelt, horn, gauges test; verify retarder switch and hoist lockout.
  5. Brakes/undercarriage: Air pressure (8 bar), pads thickness; complete/sign checklist, lockout if defects.
  6. Tailgate: Ensure latched, no damage; report overload risks from prior shift.

Assessment Criteria 2.2: Start-up and shutdown procedures are followed according to manufacturer’s specifications and worksite procedures.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Enter cab safely, buckle up; clear area, sound horn and flash lights to warn spotters.
  2. Start sequence: Key on, build air pressure (120 psi), idle 3 min; test parking brake hold.
  3. Warm-up: Engage retarder, rev to 1200 RPM; check no unusual noises before moving.
  4. Shutdown: Park designated, lower body fully, idle 5 min to cool turbo; apply brakes, key off.
  5. Post: Chock wheels if slope, lock cab, log mileage; handover to next operator.

Specific Outcome 3: Operate Rigid Body Dump Truck

Assessment Criteria 3.1: Rigid Body Dump Truck is operated safely, minimizing hazards in accordance with statutory requirements.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Route check: Confirm haul road condition, signage; position at loader with 3m clearance from edge.
  2. Loading: Align under chute, signal spotter; stop hoist if overload detected by scales.
  3. Haul: Accelerate gradually to 40km/h, use retarder on downs; maintain 50m convoy spacing.
  4. Dumping: Approach tip head slowly, raise body fully; back away after tailgate opens, check for spillage.
  5. Hazard control: Beacon on, avoid wet patches; adhere to MHSA for dust suppression and speed limits.
  6. Emergency: If brake fade, use escape ramp; activate kill switch for fires.

Assessment Criteria 3.7: Statutory requirements including relevant codes of practice and safety/health/environment legislation are listed and explained.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Obey signs: Aligns with OHS Act for hazard awareness and collision avoidance on multi-lane roads.
  2. Established roads: Supports ISO 14001 by reducing erosion and fuel waste off-route.
  3. Keep left/overtaking: Ensures MHSA traffic rules for safe passing without blind-spot risks.
  4. Speed limits (40km/h): Minimizes rollover per equipment codes, enhancing control.
  5. 50m distance: Provides reaction buffer as per safety standards for large vehicles.
  6. Yield laden: Prioritizes stability of full trucks under load regs to prevent tip-overs.

Specific Outcome 4: Document Rigid Body Dump Truck Use

Assessment Criteria 4.1: Rigid Body Dump Truck use is documented according to worksite procedures, including logs and reports.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Log cycle: Record loads hauled (e.g., 25 trips), tons moved, fuel (500L) in digital app or book.
  2. Faults: Note EMS alerts (e.g., low air), steps taken, supervisor sign-off.
  3. Maintenance: Update daily sheet with tire pressures, grease points; file for fleet audits.
  4. Incidents: Detail spills (e.g., 2 tons lost), causes, cleanup per OHS reporting.
  5. Shift end: Handover notes, e.g., "Body clean, 80% fuel, brakes checked."
