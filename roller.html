Observation Checklist for Road Roller Operation

1. Observation Checklist

Specific Outcome 1: Demonstrate Knowledge of the Functions of a Roller

Assessment Criteria 1.1: The main function of the Roller is explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Explain primary purpose: To compact soil, gravel, or asphalt layers on road bases or surfaces by applying static or vibratory force, achieving specified density for stability and drainage.
  2. Identify key attachments: Smooth drum for initial passes (e.g., 1.8m width), padfoot for cohesive soils; ensure vibration isolators are intact to prevent uneven compaction.
  3. Describe operational limits: Max grade 8%, speed 4km/h vibrating; reference manual to avoid drum damage on sharp turns.
  4. Highlight safety: Functions align with site procedures to minimize over-compaction leading to cracking or machine tip-over on soft ground.
  5. Demonstrate on machine: Point to drum controls and water system to illustrate compaction cycle without starting, emphasizing sprinkler activation for asphalt to prevent sticking.

Assessment Criteria 1.2: Roller operation procedures are listed, and the main functions of all major components are explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. List procedures: Pre-start checks, pattern passes (e.g., 6-8 per layer), edge rolling, shutdown; follow to achieve 95% Proctor density without segregation.
  2. Explain engine: Diesel for drum drive/vibration; inspect oil (SAE 15W-40) and filters to ensure consistent amplitude (1.8mm).
  3. Explain fuel system: Supplies to engine; check tank (200L) and lines for leaks to support full-day operations.
  4. Explain hydraulic system: Powers steering/propulsion (150 bar); top-up fluid (ISO VG 32) to maintain smooth drum rotation.
  5. Explain electrical system: Gauges and vibrators; test battery (12V) and switches for reliable frequency control (30Hz).
  6. Explain vibration system: Eccentric weights for compaction; calibrate amplitude to match soil type, avoiding resonance damage.
  7. Explain water system: Sprinkles drum to cool/lubricate; fill tank and check nozzles for uniform spray during asphalt rolling.

Assessment Criteria 1.3: Maximum operating capacities of the Roller are described in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Locate capacity plate: On frame, indicating static weight (e.g., 10 tons) for initial breakdown rolling.
  2. Describe drum specs: Centrifugal force 100kN vibrating; limit to 2m width for sub-base layers.
  3. Explain charts: Pass frequency (4-6) for 98% density; adjust for moisture content (optimum 12%).
  4. Verify on-site: Set speed per chart (3km/h), test on trial area to confirm compaction without rutting.
  5. Transport limits: Total mass 12 tons; secure drums during towing to prevent shifting.

Specific Outcome 2: Start and Shut Down Roller

Assessment Criteria 2.1: Pre-operational checks are carried out according to the daily inspection checklist.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Wear PPE: Hard hat, high-vis vest, ear plugs, boots; inspect for damage to protect against vibration and noise (85dB limit).
  2. Walk-around: Check drum surfaces for flat spots, tires (if sheepsfoot) for wear, lights; look for hydraulic leaks or loose scrapers.
  3. Engine area: Verify oil/fuel/coolant; clean air intake from dust to prevent power reduction.
  4. Vibration/hydraulics: Grease bearings (NLGI 2), test eccentric weights; ensure no binding in steering.
  5. Water system: Fill tank (500L), check pump and nozzles for clogs to maintain spray during hot weather.
  6. Complete checklist: Sign off, report defects (e.g., low drum pressure) before startup.

Assessment Criteria 2.2: Start-up and shutdown procedures are followed according to manufacturer’s specifications and worksite procedures.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Enter cab, adjust seat/mirrors, fasten belt; clear area, sound horn to alert workers near compaction zone.
  2. Start engine: Key on, check warnings, idle 3 min; engage vibration only after 1000 RPM warm-up.
  3. Test run: Forward/reverse at low speed (2km/h), verify steering response; activate water spray for first pass.
  4. Shutdown: Park on firm ground, disengage vibration, idle 3 min; apply parking brake, key off, exit with keys.
  5. Post: Drain water if freezing, lock controls, log hours; brief next operator on fuel status.

Specific Outcome 3: Operate Roller

Assessment Criteria 3.1: Roller is operated safely, minimizing hazards in accordance with statutory requirements.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Site prep: Survey layer thickness (150mm max), erect barriers; start at edges to prevent overhang slumping.
  2. Compaction pattern: 4 static + 4 vibrating passes; overlap 0.3m, maintain straight lines for uniform density.
  3. Speed control: 3km/h vibrating, turn off vibration on turns >15° to avoid cracking; monitor for hot spots.
  4. Hazard mitigation: Beacon/horn active, 20m clearance from excavations; comply with OHS Act vibration limits (5m/s²).
  5. Finish: Test density with nuclear gauge (95% min), cleanup edges; avoid over-rolling causing plastic deformation.
  6. Emergency: If loss of traction, stop immediately; use kill switch for hydraulic failures.

Assessment Criteria 3.2: Concerns during operation are identified and addressed (e.g., temperature increase).

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Gauge monitoring: If temp >80°C, reduce speed/idle to cool; inspect for clogged radiator fins.
  2. EMS Class A: Immediate stop, shutdown, tag out; notify supervisor for urgent repair.
  3. Class B: Log fault, continue cautiously, schedule end-of-shift fix (e.g., low vibration amplitude).
  4. Class C: Note for weekly service; monitor closely, e.g., minor water leak.
  5. Report: Detail in log (e.g., "Overheat at pass 5, cooled 10 min"), actions taken for audit.

Specific Outcome 4: Document Roller Use

Assessment Criteria 4.1: Roller use is documented according to worksite procedures, including logs and reports.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Log passes: Record area covered (e.g., 2000m²), layers compacted, density achieved in daily sheet.
  2. Defects: Enter EMS issues (e.g., "Vibration fault, stopped at 10:30"), corrective steps.
  3. Service notes: Update checklist with water usage (300L), grease applied; retain for ETQA review.
  4. Incidents: Document near-misses (e.g., edge slip), analysis, preventive measures per OHS Act.
  5. Handover: Note status, e.g., "Fuel 70%, drum clean, no issues; next layer ready."
