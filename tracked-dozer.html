Observation Checklist for Tracked Dozer Operation

Unit Standard: Operate a Tracked Dozer 116058 | NQF Level: 2 | Credits: 24

1. Observation Checklist

Specific Outcome 1: Demonstrate Knowledge of the Functions of a Tracked Dozer

Assessment Criteria 1.1: The main function of the Tracked Dozer is explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Explain primary purpose: To push, rip, and level large volumes of earth or rock on construction sites, using the blade for dozing and tracks for traction in rough terrain.
  2. Identify key attachments: Semi-U blade (e.g., 4.5m width, 15m³ capacity), single-shank ripper for breaking hardpan; ensure tilt cylinder is functional for precise grading.
  3. Describe operational limits: Max blade pitch 25°, ground speed 12km/h; reference load charts to avoid track slip on slopes over 15%.
  4. Emphasize safety integration: Functions must follow site rules to prevent blade strikes or burial in unstable material during ripping.
  5. Demonstrate on machine: Point to blade hydraulics and ripper beam to show dozing cycle without engine, noting pin wear indicators.

Assessment Criteria 1.2: Tracked Dozer operation procedures are listed, and the main functions of all major components are explained in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. List procedures: Pre-operation inspection, blade positioning, ripping sequence, post-shift cleanup; adhere to sequence for efficient material movement without rework.
  2. Explain engine: Turbo-diesel (e.g., 400hp) for track power; check oil (SAE 15W-40) and coolant to sustain high-load dozing.
  3. Explain fuel system: 600L tank with dual filters; inspect for contamination to avoid injector clogs during extended shifts.
  4. Explain hydraulic system: Controls blade/ripper (250 bar); monitor level (ISO VG 46) and hoses for leaks to ensure responsive tilting.
  5. Explain electrical system: Dash alarms and starter; verify battery (24V) and alternator for reliable operation in remote areas.
  6. Explain track/final drive: Powers movement; lubricate idlers (MOLYTEX EP2), check tension (30-40mm sag) for minimal wear.
  7. Explain cooling system: Radiator for engine/ hydraulics; clean debris daily to prevent overheating during ripping in dry conditions.

Assessment Criteria 1.3: Maximum operating capacities of the Tracked Dozer are described in accordance with manufacturer’s specifications.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Locate capacity plate: On cab post, showing max drawbar pull (e.g., 200kN) to determine pushing limits safely.
  2. Describe blade capacity: Carry 12m³ loose material; adjust for density (e.g., 1.8 t/m³ rock) to avoid overload.
  3. Explain performance charts: Ripper penetration 0.6m max; factor ground conditions for torque (80% rated).
  4. On-site verification: Read chart for current setup, e.g., ripper down for hardpan, ensuring no exceedance of track pressure (50kPa).
  5. Transport limits: Gross weight 40 tons; lower blade for rail/low-loader compliance.

Specific Outcome 2: Start and Shut Down Tracked Dozer

Assessment Criteria 2.1: Pre-operational checks are carried out according to the daily inspection checklist.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Don PPE: Hard hat, high-vis, steel boots, gloves; verify condition to guard against flying debris during ripping.
  2. Visual inspection: Examine tracks for damage (sprocket wear <20%), blade edges, ripper shanks; check for oil leaks on undercarriage.
  3. Engine bay: Oil/fuel/coolant levels; inspect turbo hoses and air cleaner for dust to prevent engine surge.
  4. Hydraulics/tracks: Grease blade pins (MOLYTEX EP2), adjust track tension (35mm sag); test final drive mounts.
  5. Cab/controls: Seatbelt, horn, gauges; ensure ROPS is certified for rollover protection.
  6. Sign checklist: Document defects (e.g., Class A ripper tooth missing), tag machine if unsafe.

Assessment Criteria 2.2: Start-up and shutdown procedures are followed according to manufacturer’s specifications and worksite procedures.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Mount cab safely, adjust mirrors; clear zone, sound horn and activate beacon to signal movement.
  2. Start engine: Key on, monitor pre-lube, idle 5 min; test blade lift to full height without creep.
  3. Warm-up: Engage low gear, check track response; verify ripper penetration at idle.
  4. Shutdown: Park level, lower blade/ripper fully, idle 5 min; apply parking brake, key off, remove keys.
  5. Post-shutdown: Install stop blocks on slopes, lock cab, report hours/fuel; clean cab from dust.

Specific Outcome 3: Operate Tracked Dozer

Assessment Criteria 3.1: Tracked Dozer is operated safely, minimizing hazards in accordance with statutory requirements.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Site setup: Survey for utilities/banks, barricade area; position dozer facing downhill for stability on cuts.
  2. Dozing technique: Pitch blade 10° up, push in straight lines (50m passes); feather tracks to avoid side-slip.
  3. Ripping: Lower shank gradually to 0.5m depth; back-drag spoil to fill low spots, maintaining 3m edge distance.
  4. Hazard control: Slow to 5km/h near obstacles, use spotter; comply with MHSA for dust wetting and noise (90dB).
  5. Travel: Retract ripper, max 10km/h on roads; check path for soft spots to prevent bogging.
  6. Emergency: If track derail, shutdown and chock; signal for winch recovery without digging out.

Assessment Criteria 3.2: Statutory requirements including relevant codes of practice and safety/health/environment legislation are listed and explained.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Obey signs: Ensures OHS Act compliance for hazard zones, preventing unauthorized entry during dozing.
  2. Use established roads: Aligns with ISO 14001 to minimize soil disturbance and erosion control.
  3. Keep left unless overtaking: Follows MHSA traffic protocol for safe convoy on haul paths.
  4. Adhere to speed (10km/h): Reduces momentum risks per mobile equipment codes.
  5. Maintain 40m distance: Allows stopping buffer as per safety standards for heavy dozers.
  6. Yield to loaded vehicles: Prioritizes stability under load management guidelines.

Specific Outcome 4: Document Tracked Dozer Use

Assessment Criteria 4.1: Tracked Dozer use is documented according to worksite procedures, including logs and reports.

Standard Operating Procedures:
  1. Record metrics: Log cubic meters pushed (e.g., 500m³), ripper passes, fuel used in shift logbook.
  2. Report defects: Note Class B issues (e.g., blade tilt lag), actions, supervisor approval.
  3. Maintenance entries: Update checklist with track adjustments, grease points; file for compliance audits.
  4. Incident details: Document near-misses (e.g., bank slide), witnesses, mitigations per OHS Act.
  5. Handover: Inform relief on status, e.g., "Ripper tooth replaced, 60% fuel, soft spot marked."
